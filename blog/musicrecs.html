Week 6: Frozen Plasma - Artificial [2006]

Authored: Sun Sep 20 01:00:00 2020

I think for the next month I’ll share some things I listened to on repeat in my teenage years.

We’ll start with Frozen Plasma – Artificial, (available on spotify), which is an album I listened to on repeat in 2009-2010. I was experiencing the real world for the first time, and relating to these tracks is part of what got me through that period of my life.

Honestly, all of the songs on this album are great, despite Unborn Faith not seeming thematically appropriate.

But you guys want to know my top 3, not hear about my personal problems. Here it is, for your viewing pleasure.

  1. Frozen Plasma - A Second of Life
  2. Frozen Plasma - Crossroads
  3. Frozen Plasma - A Generation of The Lost

Honestly, all of the songs on this album are great, despite Unborn Faith not seeming thematically appropriate.

Week 4: Nine Inch Nails - Pretty Hate Machine [1989]

Authored: Sun Sep 06 01:00:00 2020

Hopefully, I’m at least fashionable late on this one.

I would like to put a spotlight on Nine Inch Nails – Pretty Hate Machine. (available on spotify). This is one of the albums that shaped modern day Industrial music, and is an excellent album in it’s own right.

Here are my top 3 tracks from the album:

  1. Nine Inch Nails - Something I Can Never Have
  2. Nine Inch Nails - That’s What I Get
  3. Nine Inch Nails - Sin
  4. Nine Inch Nails - Head Like a Hole

Honorable mention to Head Like a Hole for feeding that dark part of my mind that always wants revenge.

This is one of my go-to albums to throw on in the background and has been for a couple of years now. Usually it takes me a month to get over a new musical obsession. But I’m still stuck on this one. I’ll let that speak for itself.

Week 3: Fractured - Beneath The Ashes [2011]

Authored: Fri Aug 28 01:00:00 2020

I’m a day late and a dollar short…again.

Fractured – Beneath The Ashes, (available on spotify) is what is catching my attention this week, and I’m here to spread the fun.

Here are my top 3 tracks from this album.

  1. Fractured - Dig
  2. Fractured - Save Me
  3. Fractured - For What?
  4. Fractured - You Are (The Voice Inside My Head)

I could go on and on about what it is about this album I like. I won’t do that, because that would be boring, and suck. Just know that I will one day overpay to import a copy of this from Europe. Considering how broke I normally am, that should be endorsement enough.

Dishonorable mention to “Transendental Rage for the Fundamentals” for inciting people to give in to their inner anger and do unforgivable shit. This is the only reason I cannot rate this album the maximum possible rating, 10 out of 10. As it is, I would say it’s 9.25 out of 10 for me.

Honorable mention to “You Are (The Voice Inside My Head)” for being relatable, if creepy.

Week 2: 9mm - On The Edge [2005]

Authored: Thu Aug 20 01:00:00 2020

I meant to post about this yesterday, but forgot. :<

This week, I would like to shine some light on 9mm – On The Edge. (available on spotify)

Picking my top tracks from this album was even more difficult than last week’s album in part because this feels like it was meant to be listened to back to back, that each song builds on the one before and lays ground for the next song.

That said, I eventually settled on these three.

  1. 9mm - Senseless Words
  2. 9mm - Endless Walk
  3. 9mm - Don’t Forget
  4. 9mm - So Scared

With an honorable mention to “So Scared” for setting the tone of the album.

This is “officially” labelled Pop Punk, but in my experience, if you liked commonly known “emo” bands like My Chemical Romance, Fall Out Boy, and Panic! At The Disco, you might like this.

Authored: Wed Aug 12 01:00:00 2020

This is a rewrite. Previously I had posted particularly dense statistics.

The album I’d like to highlight this week is No Title- by Reol and GigaP. (available on spotify)

  1. Hibikase (Or: ヒビカセ)
  2. O.T.N -Big Death Edition- (Or: ギガンティック)
  3. drop pop candy

In my honest opinion, the entire album is great, and certainly worth your time.

As a side note, this is the album I prefer to listen to when playing high-paced games, as it helps me keep time and isn’t too distracting.
