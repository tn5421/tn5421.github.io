While range is clearly no problem for you, you might want to try summoning the correct summon for each monster; Slug for Nin, Snake for Gen, B.D. for Tai. As for your level problem, I suggest trying to get at least 3 referral points, so that you can get a Helper Pin: +1 Levels, and Coolness Badge: +2 Levels. If you have Cobra-Kai try activating it instead since a higher range means a higher probability of a higher attack. Be sure to use a Team that applies to it, regardless. About summons: USE THEM ANYWAYS, IT HELPS GET ROLLS HIGHER AND HIGHER. ((Sorry for flaming)) Anyways, you're going to want as high of a range as you can get since even 10 rolls of 10 is still 100 whereas 10 rolls of 14 is 140 so range counts a lot more, and you're well set with the level factor.

Use these teams:

Tai: Terri Lv.3, Bruce Jr. Level 2, & Emosuke Level 3.

Gen: Trapchan & Z-Dog OR Flutie Level 2, Trapchan, & Threads.

Nin: Venus, Sticky Level 2, & Yuri Level 2.

mikelorus 25 Jan 2009 19:02

Above advice is not very great. Your items look like you can clear most, if not all the rolls, so in that case you want to keep your blood at bankai since levels determine your damage output. If you are trying to get a drop then have T3 for whatever kaiju it is. From there, you have to evaluate your position. Is there only one kaiju? Are you planning on bashing for an extended amount of time with the stamina you have?
If there is only one and you plan to kill it and want the drop, then add T3 then the appropriate wasteland allies (the +8 lvl/range ones, and the +3lvl/+3 range ones). If you are making every single success flawlessly, then add the +10 lvl final wasteland ally.
If your village is going on for extended periods of time, then you want a more generic team. This may not maximize your damage, but you will get the most out of your stamina. I would advise T3, Straw3, and blind fury.
You can go above your tolerance to a full 12 twists for a lot more damage output as well.

Lear 25 Jan 2009 21:19

What some here seem to be forgetting is that most people who can do 350+ damage/hit aren't missing their rolls. They have strengths of +9 or +10, which means every roll is a success. They maximize rolls, not strength or range. (Hell, they already have 30+ range anyway.)

If you want to do max damage, just put together as many damage modifiers and level boosters as you can. If your strength isn't high enough that you can be sure you'll hit with every roll, then maybe you should focus on finding what items you're missing that would give you a higher strength.

By the way, if you want to maximize hits, Strawberry Sundae.

If you want to maximize damage, Flutie/Sticky/Tubby, Chunks/Palmface/Threads, Terri 3. If you have the appropriate EotS ally, switch him in for Terri 3/Tubby (if monster is Tai or you want to keep counting coup).

I usually use Strawberry3, Blindfury, and Terri 3 when I'm fighting monsters, but that's just my preference. In case you're wondering, I've averaged 143 damage per strike against the most recent monster with this set up. That's without Hard Corps or Juice bonuses. With those I could do 400+ damage per strike fairly easily.
