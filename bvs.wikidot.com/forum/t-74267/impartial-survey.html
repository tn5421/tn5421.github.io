Okay, so you're really dealing with two separate, but related issues. The first one is duplicate IP nerfing and the other is Terri 3's Counting Coup ability. The first is done to prevent muleing, where Char1 ingredient hunts and dumps them in the storehouse for Char2. How McMasters is handling it is not ideal, but it is better than anything else I've heard suggested. That horse is dead, please stop beating it. This is one of McMaster's core design philosophies, and you're not going to change his mind on it.

On the other hand, it doesn't sound like you or your girl friend are even trying to meet him half way. There are dozens if not hundreds of ways for you to get around this problem. Go to your cyber cafe/local library/college or high school campus/place of employment/free wireless hotspot/friend's house and do BvS there. My library has a strict no games policy when using their internet terminals. It's so strict, that I've never seen it enforced. I live in a place that aspires one day to qualify as 'The Boonies' and this is still an option for me, so I know it is for you.

Now for Terri 3. I think you have a fundamental misconception of how her ability works. Essentially, there are now 3 potential drops from kaiju (except for Spirit of the Sand/Nine Tailed Fox. Not sure it it's one or two for them now), the normal one, the Kage one, and the counting coup one. A high level player coming in and kicking EvilTaxi once in the tires and getting the counting coup drop in no way affects the likelihood of another player getting the normal drop. If anything, it enhances it since that powerful player is only likely to attack once. Is it fair to lower leveled players? Yes. That person with Terri 3 paid his dues (also known as Season 1) and leveled her. Every MMORPG works on a seniority system, except if you work hard, you may eventually surpass someone who's played the game longer. If you still think that's not fair, then you must also think that themes, loopable items, and season 2+ exclusive content are also unfair.

Now I'm going to tell you something. I know you've heard it before, but I'm going to tell you again because it's important. The Kaiju Drop is irrelevant. You fight Kaiju because it's your job. You are ninja. You're getting some nice stamina bonuses, the Ingredient Hunt Special Mission is available to you, and let's not forget about The Party House, all thanks to your village. It's sweet, but it ain't free.

When a Kaiju attacks you are the first and only line of defense. It's your job. I've personally sunk 63 hits and probably close to 200 000 Ryo worth of stuff on a single kaiju with no chance of getting the drop. Part of being a ninja is protecting your village from the rampages of prepubescent girls armed with magic wands on a sugar high. It's all part of the cost of keeping your party house intact.

Lear 14 Jul 2008 17:52

Several hundred suggestions are only going to annoy him, not make him change everything. I'd suggest you stop that. And considering that you really don't know much about the system that the game runs on (no one does but McM), they're probably mostly wrong.

And he does have something to track theives, but it's very hard to determine whether there are actually two distinct players, or 1 player playing two characters. If you ever dumped something in the storehouse and your girlfriend's character picked it up, how would he be able to know that was two players and not merely one player switching an item between his alts? It's too difficult, and so to avoid that, he's discouraged people from playing in the same village as someone with their same IP.

He's not trying to make the game MORE fair, not less. If he allowed players to have characters of the same IP in the same village with no penalty whatsoever, cheating would be rampant. He'd be able to catch flagrant cheating, like using one character as an obvious mule/farmer, but there would be a ton of subtle things that would go unnoticed. The system can't catch everything, and he can't monitor everyone's activities 24/7. The few who cheat would have a huge advantage on players like me, who've played entirely according to the rules.

He's limiting people's freedom slightly so that everyone has a fair chance at the game. It's not that he's being entirely unflexable; you're just not seeing how flexible he is. He could very well say, "One character per IP, and that's final," but he doesn't. Quite frankly, running the game would probably be a lot easier if he did that. He wouldn't have to code all these safeguards to make sure no one creates a mule character or that there's too much collaboration between alts, but he did. He wants to allow people the most freedom they can have in the game, but there are inevitably some rules that he has to put in place, else anarchy would rule.

Also, the rule that duplicate IPs should be in different villages helps protect you. If you're in the same village, you could get caught for cheating that's not really cheating. He doesn't want to accidentally ban people who are just trying to play the game fairly, and so he's told you what you should do to protect yourself.

He has given you plenty of room as far as duplicate IPs go. If you're in different villages, then there'll be no problem. If you choose to be in the same village, then you're going to have to deal with all the anti-cheating rules he's put in place. That's just the way it is.

And remember, all (or at least most) of these rules are because someone has abused something in the past. He's merely making sure that it doesn't happen again. Writing a code to stop ALL cheating, while at the same time NEVER catching someone who isn't cheating AND allowing duplicate IPs in the same village would be infinitely meticulous and probably just not possible. Also, considering that he's still adding content to the game, I don't think he wants to spend several hours working on some issue that he's already solved.

So, you really have three options:

1.) One of you move to a new village. Slightly inconvenient, but not a horrible sacrifice.

2.) One of you start playing from a different IP. It's really not too hard. Ren Po Ken offered several solutions to that problem.

3.) Deal with the restrictions. Alternate monster attacks. You attack one monster, then she attacks the next monster, and so on. Alternate ingredient collection days and buy a trial mix every other day to make up for it. Stay out of the storehouse and don't trade items between each other.
