Re: Winning in the arena?
BvS-Uzuki 07 May 2008 02:41

There's lots of different ways to approach it, but to be honest, things are a lot harder than when I first started. Lots more people are out there with Bloodline powers. I've heard with zero powers, you'll probably only have a 40% chance of winning in most cases.

Personally, I play randomly if there's no strong position. When I say randomly, I mean really randomly. I use the random number generator at http://random.org/ Sometimes you'll lose in 3 rounds if you get unlucky. Usually it'll go on for a few rounds. The game doesn't really get interesting until you get to rounds 5-9, where each side is limited much more in what they can do.

But I've had a Legacy power for a while, and I just got my RedEye power as well. Powers make a huge difference.

Ideally, you should start figuring out how to get your first bloodline power (and that requires getting 10 wins). 20k ryo, 2 emergency rations (and a hollow leg to use them) is a good start. You'll probably need a stack of reputation points to go the rest.

Jadian 07 May 2008 03:02

A number of reputation points is a very good supplement to what you can't get of what Uzuki mentioned. I managed to get my first bloodline item with about 7k and about 1500 reputation points total a few days ago.

Another helpful tip is to not try going 'all the way' on a day where after 2000 ryo, you only have say, 2-3 wins ( I decided when I had 3.5 wins from 4 matches, lucky start ). There is a fair ammount of luck to it, but starting out with an advantage is better than hoping you'll get what you need with the more expensive options.

Also, while there are a lot of people with Bloodline powers by this time, most of the people I run into still don't have any. Given 4 or so matches a day, I would guess that I've only been running into one, maybe two people with the powers every other day.

As for techniques…whatever you come up with that really works. The first 3-4 rounds of the match are usually very random depending on who you're facing, and the rest, you end up quite limited in what you can do.

Re: Winning in the arena?
gurlag 07 May 2008 13:35

This is something I noticed, though it doesn't always work.

Watch the timer closely. Everytime a round ends, it resets to 60. Once a round ends, see if the message "Waiting for You…" is immediately there (should be around 55-60 secs in the timer). If it is, play a card that beats whatever your opponent used in the previous round (he used Tai in round 4, the above situation appears, then you should use Gen in round 5). It's probable that your opponent clicked Attack twice (which happens, at least to me), and thus, he played a card twice (once in the current round and another for the next one). This only works if you opponent actually has 2 or more of whatever card he used.

But like I said, this doesn't always work, so don't rely on it too much.
