if you're both using the same computer, as far as BvS can tell, it's 1 person. if the other person isn't playing BvS, this is a non issue. If the other person IS playing BvS, you'll be seen as alts of each other, relative to who has been on what IP, when.
so:
1. you play, roomie doesn't. no problem.
2. you play, roomie plays, different computers. no problem.
3. you play, roomie plays, same computer. maybe problem.
You're seen as alts of each other. thus:
no trading stuff between the two of you.
if you both have an alt, you're over the limit of characters per IP (3, last i checked)
bingos and invasions are by IP or something… i don't know the full details of this.
you can't spar with each other (duplicate IP! yadda yadda yadda)

there's probably more, but that's all i know, as i don't have an alt myself.

Vayne 16 Jan 2009 01:35

While the Anonymous Poster is right that you don't have anything to worry about if your roomie doesn't play BvS they do have a couple of things wrong about how IPs work. The IP that BvS tracks is the one of your connection to the internet, not of your computer(s) specifically. That means if you and your roomie are connected to the internet through the same modem and both play BvS the game will see you both as a single person, whether you both play on the same computer or whether you have your own computers. That won't be a problem as long as you have 3 or less characters between you, but if you both have alts then you will be over the max of 3 characters per IP. As an example, I have two computers that both connect to the internet through the same connection. Even though the computers are seperate from each other, because they share that connection they both have the same IP, so I can only have 3 BvS characters without getting into trouble.
