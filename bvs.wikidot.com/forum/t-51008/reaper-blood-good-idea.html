Reaper Blood = Good idea?
Lear 03 Apr 2008 02:04

Intuition tells me that a stronger reaper blood (that is, Bankai/Shikai) would always be a good thing, but I'm beginning to have doubts about it. In fact, I'm finding that (at least for a high level jonin) having even Shikai would make a character weaker for the vast majority of missions, and Bankai seems to be even worse.

I want to run some ideas by the people here, if you don't mind.

Obviously having a reaper blood that is too high for your current levels would be a bad idea. The level penalty would just make it near impossible to complete anything. (Imagine a level 35 ninja running around with an 18 level penalty from Bankai… well more like a 12 level penalty after you factor in the sword, but you get my point.) For that reason, I've been avoiding using bankai. I already have a (-1) penalty to all levels from Shikai and I'm wary of making it worse. However, I had figured that, when taking a more difficult mission (ie. A ranks or, heaven forbid, S ranks), the (+1) strength from going from Shikai to Bankai would outweigh the (-2) levels penalty. So, using the mission calculator, I ran some tests, and I was, to say the least, surprised.

In EVERY scenario I could come up with, using Bankai gave me a WORSE probibility of succeeding than I had with Shikai.

This led me to the obvious conclusion: For a jonin of my strength, Bankai is inherently WORSE than Shikai in all but perhaps a few select scenarios.

Well, the next logical step would be to say that Shikai is inherently WORSE than the Dormant that is but a faint memory of long ago by now. I didn't want to assume it, fearing that I had made some grave error in my logic, so I ran a few tests on this as well, and the results came up just as my conclusion said they would, Shikai DECREASED my chances of being successful on EVERY hypothetical* mission I tested.

(*Note: Every time I mention "hypothetical missions" I am referring to an imaginary mission with a difficulty and successes needed that I chose to suit my needs, which may or may not fit any particular mission available at the current time.)

I'm going to list some of the results from my calculations here, so everyone can see the same numbers I'm seeing. I start with a hypothetical run on the A rank, Withstand the Eyepatch of the Soul! with my current stats, and do some modifications to test other possibilities. I also test some other hypothetical missions, most of which would be significantly easier than the aforementioned mission. However, to make sure there were no holes in my theory, I also tested hypothetical missions which would be MORE difficult than the aforementioned.

Warning: The following paragraph contains a parenthesis within a parenthesis within a parenthesis. Read at your own risk.

So that everyone is aware of what stats I am using in these calculations, here they are (I am only using the Tai stat for calculations, since, theoretically, tai could be swaped for any other stat and everything would be nearly the same. (The only difference would be team, and that should be relatively minimal. Jutsu wouldn't play a signficant role because, with the exception of Kido:Nightfall, which is really only needed on Withstand the Eyepatch (at least within mission ranks D through A), there is a Gen and Nin equivilant for every particularly useful Tai jutsu.)):

My Tai Stats (including all items except Reaper Blood): 17(+3) levels, 14 range, +3 strength, 1 free success
My Team Bonus (only Tai affected): Billy Lvl. 2 + Emosuke Lvl. 2 = +4 range, +2 strength, 1 free success

Total Stats with items, team, and Dormant: 20 levels, 18 range, +3 strength, 2 free successes
Total Stats with items, team, and Shikai: 16 levels, 18 range, +5 strength, 2 free successes
Total Stats with items, team, and Bankai: 14 levels, 18 range, +6 strength, 2 free successes

In all of the following calculations, I am using the stats above which correlate to the proper reaper blood mentioned, unless I specifically state otherwise.

Starting with Wistand the Eyepatch (Ignoring Gen, since it would be near impossible to fail that part):

Jutsu used: Kido: Nightfall = +3 strength, +10 range

Dormant Reaper Blood = 80.3301% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 45.3567% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 17.294% chance of success

As you can see, Dormant has a significantly greater chance of success than the others. Now, testing to see if that changes under a higher difficulty.

Hypothetical mission: Difficulty:25 Successes: 15

Jutsu used: Kido: Nightfall = +3 strength, +10 range

Dormant Reaper Blood = 34.2593% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 10.4046% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 2.3562% chance of success

None of them have a high chance of success (as expected), but Dormant still beats out Shikai by a ratio of more than 3:1. Now testing to see if Reaper Blood is worth it when more Successes are needed:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 20 Successes: 17

Jutsu used: Kido: Nightfall = +3 strength, +10 range

Dormant Reaper Blood = 45.7367% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 7.8973% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 0% chance of success

This one's even worse for Reaper Blood. Dormant still has nearly a 50/50 chance of passing, whereas Shikai is a long shot, and Bankai is simply impossible. I will admit however, those missions were pretty Tai specific, since that kind of range can only be attained by Kido: Nightfall (unless you've learned Emosuke Lvl. 3's jutsu). So this time testing a few missions that aren't quite as difficult as Withstand the Eyepatch. I will use no jutsu since, theoretically, using a +2 strength jutsu on a level with a difficulty of, say 15, would merely reduce that difficulty to 13 (not technically, but as long as you have the range, it shouldn't make a difference either way. You can plug it into the calculator if you don't believe me.) The same would apply to free successes. Levels added through jutsu would also be related to the successes, but in a slightly indirect way. I'll test those though, just to be sure. So, anyway, the tests:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 15 Successes:10

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.7412% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.253% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 98.3039% chance of success

Again, Dormant wins. It's a slight difference, but enough to make you loose an extra mission every couple of days. Testing on a mission that's more success heavy:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 10 Successes: 15

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.5636% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 97.6369% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 91.2642% chance of success

Dormant is still the highest. Again, a slight difference between Dormant and Shikai, but Bankai is a killer, making you lose roughly 1 in every 11 missions (assuming no jutsu is used). Testing a higher difficulty that also has a high number of successes:

Hypothetical mission: Difficulty: 15 Successes: 15

Dormant Reaper Blood = 70.2917% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 40.4987% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 16.466% chance of success

Okay, I take back what I said about Bankai beging a killer in the last one. This mission turns a relatively safe success into a near certain failure. Hardly the benefit players are going for. Alright, now testing what happens when you add (or subtract) levels to(/from) the character, whether via jutsu or just plain leveling up (assume the old stats, just add/subtract the levels in mentioned before each hypothetical mission) I'll use a D17 S12 for the following because it seems like an upper difficulty A rank mission (upper difficulty needed because percents will be very high when I'm adding levels) :

ADD: +3 levels (Total levels = 23 w/o reaper blood)

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 17 Successes: 12

Dormant Reaper Blood = 97.0269% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 94.8629% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 91.806% chance of success

They're all high, but Dormant is the highest. Adding more levels:

ADD: +5 levels (Total levels = 25 w/o reaper blood)

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 17 Successes: 12

Dormant Reaper Blood = 98.9163% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 98.4293% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 97.6708% chance of success

Dormant is still the highest, but by a slightly lesser margin than before. Perhaps if we increase levels more Shikai and Bankai will surpass Dormant? Adding more levels:

ADD: + 10 levels (Total levels = 30 w/o reaper blood)

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 17 Successes: 12

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.9354% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.9486% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 99.9443% chance of success

Dormant is FINALLY surpassed by both Shikai and Bankai, but by this point it doesn't matter. The difference between Dormant and Shikai (which is at this point higher than Bankai) is only slightly more than 0.01%, or 1 in every 10,000 missions. Why do they finally surpass Dormant, and why is Shikai higher than Bankai? My best explanation is thus: All three, as levels are increased, eventually converge at 100%. That is to say, they never reach 100% exactly, but if you were given an infinite amount of rolls, you would have a 100% chance of passing the mission. Well, Bankai and Shikai have a higher strength number, and as the number of levels are increased, each individual level becomes more and more insignificant (compare a genin with 3 levels in a stat to a jonin with 15, 16, 18, etc.). That means that the strength comes more into play in determining success ratio, and so Bankai and Shikai converge FASTER than Dormant, but they all still converge, and for the numbers that make a real difference (at least less than 99.9% or so), it seems that Dormant will always be higher than Bankai and Shikai. Theoretically, range should also converge. It's not as intuitive as it is with levels, but I'm about 99% sure the success ratio will converge at 100% as the range becomes infinitely high. Strength will NOT converge. It will actually hit 100%, but it doesn't converge. (If you don't understand the mathematics behind that, don't worry. It's not particularly important whether a stat converges or hits 100% at a non-infinite number. The end result is essentially the same.) Just to be sure, a few more tests for lower leveled ninja. If my hypothesis is correct, Dormant should be much better in these next two calculations. I'll keep the misson difficulty and successes the same:

SUBTRACT: -3 levels (Total levels = 17 w/o reaper blood)

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 17 Successes: 12

Dormant Reaper Blood = 66.3227% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 35.6082% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 13.3348% chance of success

So far so good. Making sure the same trend occurs as more levels are decreased:

SUBTRACT: -5 levels (Total levels = 15 w/o reaper blood)

Dormant Reaper Blood = 42.5539% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 8.7233% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 3.4572% chance of success

And it seems that Dormant wins for these as well. If I decrease it anymore, it'll become impossible for Shikai and Bankai to get the required successes, so I'll stop decreasing levels here. But what if the player was significantly weaker. For these missions, I'll drop my Emosuke/Billy team. I'll ignore the Ring of Power I have as well, just to simplify things.

NEW STATS (w/o reaper blood): 20 levels, 14 range, 3 strength

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 12 Successes: 7

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.9121% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.8356% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 99.6849% chance of success

Dormant still wins in this situation. Perhaps if I only had a range of 12.

NEW STATS (w/o) reaper blood: 20 levels, 12 range, +3 strength

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty 12 Successes: 7

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.7679% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.6653% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 99.4411% chance of success

Dormant still wins. What if I had no strength boosters now? What if I was just a level 60 jonin with no permanent items (or rather, a level 51 jonin with the sword and soul glove)?

NEW STATS (w/o reaper blood): 20 levels, 12 range

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 12 Successes: 7

Dormant Reaper Blood = 94.2341% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 93.2261% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 90.7571% chance of success

Still Dormant Wins. Okay, what if I was only a level 36 (27 plus sword and glove) chunin?

NEW STATS (w/o reaper blood): 12 levels, 10 range

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 10 Successes: 7

Dormant Reaper Blood = 52.693% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 16.9127% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 0% chance of success

Still, Dormant is by far the best. Bankai doesn't even have enough levels to succeed at the mission. Now, I've tried high difficulty missions, high success mission, but not paritularly low difficulty or low success missions. Those will be next:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 5 Successes: 6

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.6097% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 96.1908% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 73.5092% chance of success

Still Dormant is better, perhaps if the difficulty were lowered one more:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 4 Successes: 6

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.9328% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.4212% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 100% chance of success

At this point, Bankai is the bes because quite frankly, it's impossible to fail. However, Dormant is more than 99.9%, so there's still a very small difference between the two. Now what if the number of successes needed was very low?

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 9 Successes: 2

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.9681% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.871% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 99.5361% chance of success

Dormant still is the best. Lets see if it makes any difference if it's one success:

Hypothetical Mission: Difficulty: 10 Successes: 1

Dormant Reaper Blood = 99.9931% chance of success
Shikai Reaper Blood = 99.9775% chance of success
Bankai Reaper Blood = 99.9271% chance of success

Dormant is still the highest, though at this point (and with the previous as well) it's basically an auto-win.

I could test other scenarios, but there's nothing that I could test that would really show us anything that can't be deduced from the other calculations. I can't reduce the levels any more because that would make Bankai and eventually Shikai give you so few levels you just can't complete the missions anymore. Decreasing the difficulty any more would still result in a >99.99% chance of success, something I'm certainly not complaining about, and you can't go lower than 1 success. I think I've already adequately tested the higher range, with a few that had a very high difficulty and some with a very high amount of successes. I also think I've adequately hit the different focuses of missions (ie. low success, high difficulty versus high success, low difficulty), so I don't think there's much more to check there.

Basically, I'm forced to conclude that Reaper Blood - Dormant is, in every possible situation that actually matters, superior to both Reaper Blood - Shikai and Reaper Blood - Bankai.

So, to those of you (if there are any) that had the patience to actually read all that, I pose the following question: Can you prove me wrong? I don't think so, but you never know. Maybe there is a character out there who actually benefits from Reaper Blood. I may have wasted the past 3 hours working on my theory, and if so, I'd like to hear your refutation to my theory.

Otherwise, I think McMasters, despite his infinite wisdom, has made a tiny blunder, and accidentally created an item that actually WEAKENS your character. Or maybe it wasn't an accident.

Edit: I should note that all of my calculations are from the calculator provided at http://bvswiki.com/utility:calculator. If there's some flaw with that calculator then, of course, all of my calculations are off, and this entire post becomes pointless. I'm placing my faith in Ren Po Ken's mathematical/programming abilities here.
