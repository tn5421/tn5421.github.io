On Duplicate Monster Drops
BvS-Uzuki 23 May 2008 01:31

So, I was thinking about a new system which is a little less arbitrary than our current system when dealing out duplicate monster drops. Here's what I was thinking…

Whenever you successfully plant a spy, you get +3 points
Whenever you successfully invade, you get +5 points
Whenever you attack a monster, you get +2 points

You keep your points until you get a monster drop. Whenever there's a duplicate monster drop that I can give out, whoever has the highest points currently gets first choice at it. They can pass to the next person, until eventually someone takes it. The points awarded as roughly the amount of stamina spent helping the village.

Doing it this way would (1) reward people who help the village out the most get more monster drops, rather than the guy who just happened to get lucky on this particular summon, and (2) spread the monster drops out a little more evenly, (3) encourage people to wack at monsters even if they don't want that particular drop.

I've got a script that can parse the combat log for these events and award points based on them. Effectively, I can parse the chatlog for the events, then delete them, then post the updated numbers to the forums so everyone knows where everyone else stands at any given time.

Thoughts?

Lear 23 May 2008 18:09

It's too complicated, and it encourages people to plant spys arbitrarily.

Also, it makes it way too easy to fall very far behind. Lets say a storm riders attacks while I'm unable to attack it any more than twice, or perhaps even at all. So, lets say that 10 people each get 14 attacks at it, and I only got 4. That means that 10 people each got 10 more attacks, or 20 more points, putting me pretty far behind. If they keep attacking, I can be reasonably sure that I won't get any of the next 10 drops, which would discourage more than encourage in my opinion. Plus, new people to the village will always start in last, which would discourage them from trying since they're already in 50th place for the next monster drop. Yes, it does guarantee that everyone will eventually get one as long as they keep doing stuff, but so does the current system really. We haven't been fighting a lot of monsters for a very long time. As long as there aren't a bunch of jerks that keep trying to get items they already have, then you can pretty much guarantee you'll get a drop eventually as long as you're fighting monsters.

The reason why? Look at it like this:

Lets say a monster attacks this thread, and we all fight to defend the thread. The number of attacks are thus:

naito: 9
Schon: 7
Gogolu: 6
Ananvil: 4
Uzuki: 4
Jupiah: 3
Lear: 3

So, Uzuki gets the drop because he's the thread starter and Schon gets one because the random number generator likes him best.

A few days later, the same monster attacks. And we attack as thus:

naito: 10
Gogolu: 7
Ananvil: 6
Jupaih: 5
Lear: 4
Uzuki: 3
Schon: 1

So this time, naito gets the random drop, and Uzuki gives the drop to Gogolu.

Another attack:

Ananvil: 14
Jupiah: 12
Lear: 11
naito: 3
Uzuki: 3
Gogolu: 1
Schon: 0

Here, Jupiah gets the random drop, but Ananvil still gets one from Uzuki. At this point, there's only 1 without a drop, and I can be fairly certain to get it the next time.

Given, this is small scale; I'm using 7 villagers, not 50, but also remember, not everyone in our village seriously attacks a monster with the intent of getting the drop, so the real pool will be less than 50.

Also, keep in mind that, with the exception of the Sand Demon and the Nine-tailed Fox, each time we defeat a monster, 2 items are given out, so even with a pool of 50, after fighting a monster 25 times, everyone should have the drop.

The system that's in place now does work, and it should balance out in the end. It might not seem like it now because it hasn't been very long, but give it some time, and it should.

Also, Jupiah, I don't think he wants to put that much effort into tracking everything people do, and donating ryo for points basically means you can buy drops.
