3/22 - NEW: Upgrade Planning
BvS-Uzuki 22 Mar 2008 21:04

Time for an update! We've been in the NWW for 9 days now, and, quite frankly, we've been kicking butt. We've hit some of our major milestones (we've got a Jonin Council, War Room, Potion Palace, and a handful of defensive upgrades as well), and I think we're getting closer to the point where we can move onto the next stage.

I think we should seriously consider getting a Natural Research Facility soon. Once we get to this point, we can gain access to summons, monsters, S-Rank missions, and numerous other important upgrades. However, the competition becomes much more cut throat at this point.

After having seen our own spying attempts, it's becoming increasingly clear to me that defense upgrades don't do a whole hell of a lot in the grand scheme of things. We should keep working on them, but truthfully, I don't think they'll really stop us from getting hit all that regularly. The only thing that will is Peacetime bonuses. We've also reached the size where Solidarity and Scattered Outposts will do nothing for us.

Having said all that, I don't want to get an NRF until we get the following upgrades:

  • SWATBU
  • Creepy Traps
  • Science! Workshop

I think SWATBU will have the greatest reduction in attacks against us, due to the removal of permanent spies (can't hit where you have no spies). Creepy Traps just acts as an additional bit of deterrent. Neither of these will stop attacks outright, but then again, the only thing that will is Peacetime.

While I consider those two upgrades essential, I wouldn't mind getting the Science! Workshop as well before we enter the NRF stage.

We will, of course, continue going by the votes on each individual upgrade. But here's the trajectory I see things going.

Fax 22 Mar 2008 21:22

I'd like to see Science! prior to an NRF, actually. There's a lot of things (monochrome pheromone, make-out mood enhancer, reversing hourglass, rotating timesphere) that you can't get without one, and there're other benefits too: double collection bonuses would be something I'd tag as incredibly useful, especially with a decreasing number of Genin/Chunin. Higher level uses (such as the one that gives everyone 50 stamina, essentially a free attack for everyone in the village) also have their benefits. With an NRF, we'll also be fighting monsters. The third-tier activation, Driving Rain, will make fighting those monsters far, far easier (and in fact may make the Shikai and Bankai in the village hit the monster's DC on every roll).
